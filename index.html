WHAT IS NCT?

NCT (Neo Culture Technology) is a South Korean boy band founded by SM Entertainment and debuted in January 2016. As of April 2021, the group has 23 members ranging in age from late teens to mid-twenties and is separated into four different sub-units.

SM Entertainment founder Lee Soo-man gave a lecture titled "SMTOWN: New Culture Technology 2016" at the SM Coex Artium in January 2016. The label intended to launch a new boy group that would "fit in line" with its culture contents approach, with a "infinite" number of members debuting in distinct sub-units inside the group. In addition, the members would establish "sub-unit teams" and "collaborate" with one another. The group's name was eventually revealed to be NCT, an abbreviation for the title of the presentation. NCT was SM Entertainment's first idol group to debut in over two years, since Red Velvet in 2014, and its first male group to debut since Exo in 2012.On April 9, 2016, the group unveiled their first sub-unit, the rotating NCT U, with the double digital single "The 7th Sense" and "Without You." It was succeeded by the Seoul-based sub-unit NCT 127's eponymous extended play on July 7, 2016 and the then-teenaged sub-unit NCT Dream's digital single "Chewing Gum" on August 24, 2016. WayV, a seven-member sub-unit located in China and NCT's first foreign sub-unit, debuted on January 17, 2019 with the single album The Vision. NCT's second foreign sub-unit, NCT Hollywood, will be located in the United States and will make its debut at a later date.

Despite the fact that each sub-unit advertises independently, NCT has come together three times as a group to produce three full-length albums, Empathy (2018), Resonance (2020), and Universe (2020). NCT U

NCT U is the boy group NCT's first sub-unit. NCT U does not have set members, therefore the lineup for each comeback, OST, or dance stage changes based on who best suits the theme of the song. NCT U made their SM Entertainment debut on April 9th, 2016.

NCT 127

NCT 127 is the NCT's first fixed and second overall subunit. Taeil, Taeyong, Yuta, Jaehyun, Winwin, Mark, and Haechan made their debut on July 7, 2016 with the single "Firetruck" with an original roster of seven members: Taeil, Taeyong, Yuta, Jaehyun, Winwin, Mark, and Haechan. Their debut extended play (EP), NCT #127 (2016), was a commercial success upon its release, earning them the title of Best New Group at many South Korean music award ceremonies. Doyoung and Johnny joined the group in December 2016, prior to the release of their second EP Limitless (2017); Jungwoo, the last member, was debuted in September 2018 for the group's first studio album Regular-Irregular (2018).

NCT Dream

NCT Dream is NCT's third sub-unit. They were supposed to be NCT's adolescent unit before abandoning their young look and rebranding in 2020, when all members became legal adults in South Korea. The sub-unit made their debut on August 25, 2016 with the single "Chewing Gum" with a roster of seven members—Mark, Renjun, Jeno, Haechan, Jaemin, Chenle, and Jisung—with an average age of 15.6 years. Since then, NCT Dream has been regarded globally as one of the most famous young musicians of their generation, with songs representing youth and adolescence, growth, and the shift from innocence to rebellion.

WayV

WayV (Pinyin: WiShén V; an abbreviation for We Are Your Vision) is a Chinese boy band handled by SM Entertainment's Chinese sublabel, Label V, and the fourth sub-unit and China-based unit of the South Korean boy band NCT. The Vision, the group's digital extended play (EP), was released on January 17, 2019. Kun, Ten, Winwin, Lucas, Xiaojun, Hendery, and Yangyang are the band's seven members. The band has released one studio album and three EPs and has consistently charted on Billboard's international rankings. The group was acknowledged to be a sub-unit of NCT, although they were not advertised as such until NCT 2020 was announced. Journalists speculated that WayV's departure from the NCT label was due to strained ties between China and South Korea. Despite this, SM CEO Chris Lee has recognized WayV as NCT's China-based unit from its inception.
